Diwali

Title: The Festival of Lights

Description: Diwali is the most important Hindu celebration in India. It's known as the festival of lights because there are lots of lights and brightness.

Facts:

  • Diwali marks the victory of light over darkness and good over evil.
  • It is celebrated by lighting oil lamps and bursting fireworks.
  • People clean and decorate their homes for the festival.

Food:

  • Traditional sweets like ladoos and barfis are made and shared.
  • Savory snacks such as samosas and pakoras are also popular in Diwali.

Clothing

  • During Diwali, there are many types of clothing to wear. For women, there are Anarkali suits which are beautiful colorful dress-like clothes.
  • For men, there are kurtas, which are short dress-like clothing that come in lots of different colors. There are many other types of clothing to wear for diwali too.

Other Details:

  • Diwali is also a time for families to meet and give gifts to each other.
  • Rangoli, colorful patterns made on the floor, is a common decoration during Diwali.

History

Diwali is about king Rama's return to the kingdom of Ayodhya after deafeating the demon king, Ravana, showing good is better than evil.
